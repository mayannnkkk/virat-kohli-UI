virat kohli

about T20 career stats

Virat Kohli, one of the greatest modern-day cricketers, has had an illustrious career in T20 cricket. Renowned for his consistency, adaptability, and remarkable chasing ability, Kohli has been a cornerstone of India's T20 International (T20I) team since his debut in 2010. As of now, he is among the highest run-scorers in T20I history, with multiple records to his name, including being the fastest to significant milestones like 2,000 and 3,000 T20I runs. Kohli’s batting average and strike rate stand out, especially in high-pressure situations, where he has often guided India to memorable wins. He has also been a star performer in the Indian Premier League (IPL), captaining the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) and amassing over 7,000 runs, including numerous centuries and fifties. Kohli’s aggressive yet calculated approach, combined with his impeccable fitness and commitment, makes him one of the most iconic figures in T20 cricket history.

about ODI career stats

Virat Kohli has established himself as one of the greatest ODI cricketers of all time, renowned for his unparalleled consistency, adaptability, and hunger for runs. Since making his ODI debut in 2008, Kohli has become a pivotal figure in Indian cricket, leading the team to numerous victories with his match-winning performances. As of now, he is among the highest run-scorers in ODI history, with over 13,000 runs and counting, including more than 40 centuries—second only to Sachin Tendulkar. Kohli is particularly celebrated for his prowess in run chases, earning the nickname "Chase Master" for his ability to anchor innings under pressure. His leadership as India's ODI captain from 2017 to 2021 further solidified his legacy, with a remarkable win percentage. Whether through his classic cover drives or his aggressive running between the wickets, Kohli has set a benchmark for excellence in ODI cricket, inspiring millions worldwide.

virat kohli TEST

about TEST career stats

Virat Kohli’s Test cricket career is a testament to his versatility and dominance across all formats. Debuting in 2011, he has risen to become one of the finest Test batsmen of the modern era, known for his aggressive approach, impeccable technique, and insatiable hunger for runs. Kohli has scored over 8,500 Test runs, including numerous double centuries, and has consistently delivered in challenging conditions, both at home and overseas. As India's Test captain from 2014 to 2022, he redefined the team's approach, emphasizing fitness, aggression, and fast bowling dominance. Under his leadership, India achieved historic victories, including their first-ever Test series win in Australia and a climb to the No. 1 spot in the ICC Test rankings. Kohli's commitment to the longest format and his ability to perform under pressure have made him an icon in Test cricket, inspiring a new generation to value the art of the red-ball game.
